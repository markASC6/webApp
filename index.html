The internet has allowed bad actors to publish fake information that looks real with no regulation or oversight.

Most fake news is created to generate clicks and sell ads, but some is created to spread a narrative. In the 2016 election, the Russian funded the creation of fake news to promote a Donald Trump presidency. If people don’t double check an article before sharing it, fake news will only become more prevalent.

Today people can use sites like Facebook and Twitter to lock isolate themselves into political echochambers

These sites allow people to only view news from certain sources they like, or only see news that their friends share. This means they are only ever seeing points of view that agree with them. This makes it easier to believe more and more outlandish fake news articles. People are increasingly seeing the other side as the devil, so obviously fake stories that confirm this attitude aren’t given the scrutiny they deserve.

Fake news writers target stories that make readers feel strong emotions like anger, shock, and fear.
When people see a story that makes them feel a strong emotion, they don’t stop to verify if it is true. Their immediate instinct is to share it with their friends or with their followers. This adds credibility to the fake story and leads their followers to share it too. Before anyone realizes a story is fake, hundreds of people may have shared it and been enraged, shocked, or terrified by it. Verify your news before you share it.
